I make films about erased histories that refuse to be translated. I build marketing systems that turn data into narrative. I counsel queer people in places where survival itself is criminalized.

My work sits at the intersection of three seemingly incompatible worlds: computer science (COMSATS, Pakistan), business strategy (MBA, Concordia), and experimental cinema (Indus Valley School of Art). Most people see contradiction. I see methodology.

Right now, I'm training AI models at Meta Reality Labs while developing Bulleyah, a feature film recovering the erased queer identity of 18th-century Sufi poet Bulleh Shah. I'm also applying to fully-funded Visual Arts PhD programs (UCSD, UCLA, Berkeley) to formalize practice-based research that refuses Western academic extraction.

Before this? I drove 40% traffic growth as SEO Analyst at My Otaku World, managed 2,000+ content pieces, and built marketing systems at AbbVie, Cotton Connection, and Rikovations. Numbers tell stories too—when you know how to listen.

The throughline: I recover what's been suppressed. Whether it's a 300-year-old poet's queerness, a brand's hidden audience, or a teenager's first question about PrEP on Reddit—my work is about making the invisible legible without destroying its illegibility in the process.

2021 6:01 Surrealist Experimental

Mann Ki Hastam (Who Am I)

A fever dream through identity, migration, and the violence of categorization. The title asks Bulleh Shah's question: "Who am I?" The film refuses to answer. 15+ international festival selections.

On the impossibility of self-knowledge when every mirror is colonial, every archive redacted, every family photo censored. The surrealist imagery isn't metaphor—it's documentation of what survival looks like when legibility itself is weaponized.

2024 6:20 Experimental Narrative

Rekhta

Black-and-white confinement meets saturated color liberation. Named after the Urdu poetic form that broke Persian's grammatical chains, the film stages its own formal rebellion—color as escape velocity from monochrome containment.

Rekhta poetry mixed Urdu with Persian, creating a hybrid language the establishment couldn't control. This film does the same with image: when black-and-white (the colonial gaze) fails to contain the subject, color bleeds through like contraband joy.

2021 4:50 Silent Film

Hawwa Ka Beta (Thanda Gosht)

Silent adaptation of Saadat Hasan Manto's Partition-era story. No dialogue because Manto's words need no translation—and because silence forces you to watch what language hides. The title means "Eve's Son" / "Cold Flesh."

Manto wrote about Partition's sexual violence when everyone else wrote nationalist mythology. This film honors his refusal to look away. Silent cinema as ethical choice: images that testify without explaining, without comforting, without resolving.

2021 3:56 Music Video / Experimental

Pride Flag Color Theory

Experimental music video interrogating what happens when Western queer symbols cross borders. Is the rainbow universal or just another export? Can a flag designed in San Francisco speak for Karachi?

Color as contested territory. The Pride flag promises liberation but arrives as empire. This video asks: what does decolonial queerness look like? What symbols survive translation? What gets lost?

Reddit LGBTQ+ Peer Counseling

Active moderator and peer counselor in Pakistani LGBTQ+ communities where queerness is criminalized, families are violent, and institutional support doesn't exist.

What this looks like:

  • Sexual health education: PrEP access, safer sex practices, harm reduction strategies
  • Crisis intervention: First-response support for people experiencing family violence, outing threats, survival emergencies
  • Mental health support: Trauma-informed peer counseling, suicide prevention, relationship advice

This work shapes my filmmaking. You can't make honest films about queer South Asian life from a Western academic distance. The questions on Reddit at 3 AM—"Am I going to hell?" Ethical Framework: Community accountability, not extraction. Harm reduction, not moral judgment. Survival strategies, not assimilation narratives.

Why This Matters

In Pakistan, homosexuality is punishable by life imprisonment. Families commit honor killings. The state archives erase us, and Western academia extracts our stories for tenure.

My work refuses both: refuses state violence AND refuses Western academic voyeurism. It's filmmaking and community work as inseparable practices. You can't recover erased histories ethically if you're disconnected from living communities.

This isn't charity. This is survival infrastructure built by and for us.
